	\section{Description}
		
		The common schoolbook definition of the cosine of an angle $\theta$ in a right-angled triangle is given by,
		
		\begin{center}
			\Large cos $\theta$=\Large $\frac{adjacent}{hypotenuse}$
		\end{center}	    
%		\begin{center}
%			\includegraphics[width=0.35\columnwidth]{pic1.png}

		In mathematics, the inverse trigonometric functions (also called arcus function, antitrigonometric functions or cyclometric functions) are the inverse of the basic trigonometric functions (Specifically they are the inverse of sine, cosine, tangent, cotangent, secant and cosecant functions) and are used to obtain an angle from any of the angleâ€™s trigonometric ratios. Thus, similar to the definition of cosine, the arccos can be defined as,
		\begin{center}
			\Large arccos $\theta$=\Large$\frac{hypotenuse}{adjacent}$
		\end{center}
